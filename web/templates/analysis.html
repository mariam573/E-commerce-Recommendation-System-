📚 Method Explainer

What it is: Recommends items based on the idea that users who agreed in the past will agree in the future. Relies entirely on rating history.

1. User-Based Pearson

Mean-centres ratings then computes Pearson correlation between every user pair. Accounts for individual rating bias (some users always rate high/low).

2. User-Based Jaccard

Treats interactions as binary (any rating = interacted). Scores neighbours by J(A,B) = |A∩B| / |A∪B|. Designed for implicit/binary feedback.

3. Item-Based Adj. Cosine

Subtracts each user's mean before computing cosine similarity between items. More stable than user-based as item catalogues change less than user tastes.

4. MF Gradient Descent

Decomposes the user-item matrix into latent factor matrices P and Q via manual SGD. Predicted rating = P[u]·Q[i]. No built-in decomposition used.

What it is: Recommends items similar to those the user has interacted with, based solely on item attributes. No data from other users needed.

1. TF-IDF + Cosine

Each product is converted into a text document. TF-IDF vectors are computed from scratch, then compared via cosine similarity.

2. Feature Vector

Category and brand are one-hot encoded; price and rating are min-max normalised. Cosine similarity finds structurally similar products.

What it is: Applies explicit domain knowledge or user constraints. Does not rely on historical ratings.

1. Constraint-Based

Accepts hard constraints: max budget, preferred categories, brands, min rating. Surviving products ranked by overall rating.

2. Case-Based Reasoning

Takes a reference product and scores all others on weighted similarity: Category 40%, Brand 20%, Price 20%, Rating 20%.

RMSE

Root Mean Squared Error — how accurately the model predicts held-out ratings. Lower = better. Only applies to CF methods.

Precision@5

Of the top-5 recommendations, what fraction are genuinely relevant (rating ≥ 4)? Higher = better.

Recall@5

Of all items the user would rate ≥ 4, how many appear in the top-5? Higher = better.

Coverage

What % of the catalogue is recommended at least once? High coverage avoids popularity bias.

Diversity

Average pairwise cosine distance between recommended TF-IDF vectors. Higher = more varied lists.
